Inverse Kinematics of a Parallel Mechanism

IEEE EMBC 2021

This project introduces a novel 3SPS-S-3RRR parallel mechanism for upper-limb prosthetic wrists. Unlike traditional designs, our offset Kresling-inspired structure generates rotational wrist motion mechanically—eliminating the need for extra motors.

We used Denavit–Hartenberg parameters and screw theory to derive both forward and inverse kinematics. The combined system achieves quasi-spherical mobility while remaining lightweight and tendon-driven, ideal for amputees with trans-radial loss.

Our early-stage control strategy links motion capture and surface electromyography (sEMG) to regress natural user input into trajectory commands.
